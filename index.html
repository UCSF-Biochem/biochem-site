Department of Biochemistry researchers, in collaboration with St. Jude, have announced the approval of a preclinical candidate for the inhibition of parasite sodium channels. More >>>
The Department of Biochemistry is pleased to announce the opening of the Maker Space, an open access facility for the rapid prototyping and fabrication of medical and research devices. More >>>
Peter Walter has won the 2015 Vilcek award! The Vilcek Foundation awards the Vilcek Prizes annually to immigrants who have made lasting contributions to American society through their extraordinary achievements in biomedical research and the arts and humanities. Two prizes are awarded, one in biomedical research and one in the arts and humanities, in a field designated by the foundation. More >>>
Wallace Marshall, an Associate Professor in the Department of Biochemistry, has announced the first genome-wide study of Stentor, a model for cellular regeneration and growth. The Marshall lab seeks to understand how the three-dimensional geometry of cells can be encoded in a one-dimensional genome and developed into varying cellular architectures under genetic control. We are investigating this question at the level of individual organelles. More >>>
